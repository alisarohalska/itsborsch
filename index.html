Borscht: A Ukrainian Classic with a Long Story

Borscht is a beet-based soup with many regional versions, strongly connected to Ukrainian home cooking and family traditions.

History (short overview)

  • The word “borscht” is linked to older Slavic soups and historical precursors of the dish.
  • References to borscht traditions appear in historical notes connected to the Kyiv region.
  • “Culture of Ukrainian borscht cooking” is recognized by UNESCO as intangible cultural heritage in need of urgent safeguarding.

Interesting facts

  • Many people love borscht even more the next day, when the flavor has time to develop.
  • Common serving additions include sour cream, fresh herbs, and garlic buns (pampushky).
  • There is no single “one true” version—recipes change by region, season, and family style.

Classic Ukrainian Borscht (Home-Style)

This is a practical “family pot” approach: a broth base, sautéed vegetables, beet for color, and finishing touches like garlic and herbs.

Ingredients

  • Broth base: beef or pork (or a rich vegetable broth).
  • Vegetables: beets, potatoes, cabbage, carrots, onion.
  • Tomato element: tomatoes or tomato paste.
  • Aromatics: garlic, bay leaf, black pepper.
  • Finish: fresh dill/parsley and sour cream (optional).

Method (step-by-step)

  1. Simmer the broth until the meat is tender (if using meat), then season lightly.
  2. Prep vegetables: shred cabbage, dice potatoes, grate/julienne beets and carrots, chop onion.
  3. Sauté onion + carrots, then add beets; add tomato paste/tomatoes and cook until fragrant.
  4. Add potatoes and cabbage to the pot, then add the sautéed beet mixture.
  5. Add garlic and herbs near the end, adjust seasoning, and let the soup rest 10–20 minutes.
  6. Serve with sour cream and extra herbs if you like.

Innovative Approaches (Non-traditional Ideas)

Below are quick ideas: what changes from the classic version, and what extra ingredients are commonly added.

Vegetarian / Vegan borscht

  • Changed: meat broth → vegetable broth (or mushrooms for deeper flavor).
  • Added: beans for protein, extra herbs, sometimes smoked paprika.

Video: Variations & ideas (YouTube)

Smoky “campfire” style

  • Changed: fresh meat → smoked ribs/sausage (or add smoked elements to the broth).
  • Added: smoked paprika, sometimes prunes in small amounts.

Video: Traditional borscht video (YouTube)

Sweet–sour balance twist

  • Changed: stronger control of acidity and sweetness.
  • Added: a little vinegar/lemon, sometimes apple or a pinch of sugar to balance beets and tomato.

Video: Borscht background & evolution (YouTube)

Green-borscht direction (concept)

  • Changed: beet-forward profile → leafy greens (sorrel/spinach style).
  • Added: eggs (often) and lots of fresh herbs.
